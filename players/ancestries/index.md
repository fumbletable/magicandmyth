# Ancestries

Ancestry symbolizes descent and heritage. It is responsible for the varied and unique genetic codes, traditions, morals and norms that bring color to a fantasy setting.

Oddities do exist, but members of an ancestry often share common physical and emotional traits. Some of these traits have a certain synthesis with a class or two, or perhaps an outright clashing, so it is important to keep one eye on a prospective class while casting the other eye on available ancestries. This is merely a suggestion. Sometimes, the best player characters spawn from the most unique and unassuming builds.

This chapter contains seven ancestries: dwarf, elf, gnome, half-elf, half-orc, halfling and human.

## Reading the Entries

Each ancestral entry contains several categories of information, such as physical and social traits, languages, attributes, combat speed, weapons and armor, special senses and proficiencies. The information is fairly straight-forward; we provided some information to help roleplay the ancestry and other information to help you build your character.

One tidbit is optional: Ancestral Weaponry. Check with your GM to see if he or she is allowing ancestral weapon proficiencies and/or bonuses. The use of ancestral proficiencies and bonuses can be viewed as campaign-specific and the allowance of them is discretionary.

## Languages

All characters speak Common and their ancestral language, if any. Characters may also start with additional languages based on high Intelligence. These initial bonus languages should first be chosen based on the Bonus Languages Per Ancestry table below. Only with the GM's permission may a player choose a bonus language not listed in this chart.

**Highly Specialized Languages:** One can assume that any ancestry (or monster) could, if intelligent, create its own distinct language or speak an almost unrecognizable dialect of another language. For example, many drow elves use a special form of sign language they created to communicate without sound. Characters with experience dealing with such languages in their setting could start with one or more of these highly specialized languages or dialects as bonus languages.
