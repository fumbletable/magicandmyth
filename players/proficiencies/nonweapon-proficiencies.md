# Nonweapon Proficiencies

Nonweapon proficiencies (NWPs) represent skills, knowledge, and trades your character has learned. Every class starts with 3 NWP slots.

## NWP Groups by Class

Each class has access to specific NWP groups. You can spend slots on proficiencies outside your groups, but they cost more.

| Class | NWP Groups |
|-------|------------|
| Barbarian | General, Nature |
| Bard | Academic, General, Rogue |
| Cleric | Academic, General |
| Druid | Academic, General, Nature |
| Fighter | General |
| Monk | Academic, General |
| Paladin | Academic, General |
| Ranger | General, Nature |
| Thief | Academic, General, Rogue |
| Wizard | Academic, General |

## Ancestral Proficiencies

Some ancestries grant bonus proficiencies at character creation:

| Ancestry | Bonus |
|----------|-------|
| Dwarf | Minor proficiency in Engineering or Mining |
| Gnome | Minor proficiency in Gemcutting and Engineering |
| Half-Elf | One additional proficiency slot (weapon or nonweapon) |
| Half-Orc | Minor proficiency in Wilderness Survival or Mountaineering |
| Thief (class) | 4 bonus NWP slots from Rogue Skills |

## How NWP Checks Work

When using a nonweapon proficiency, roll d20 + check modifier (from the relevant attribute) against the difficulty target. The GM sets the difficulty based on the situation.
